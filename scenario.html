How to Use Atlas A First-Time User Guide

Atlas is a live scenario tool you run during or right after a client call. Fill in what you know, and the products and payment comparison update instantly. Here's how to move through it.

Step 1 — Start a New Call

Click New Call in the top-right to clear all fields and reset the timer. Do this at the start of every conversation so you're working with a clean slate.

The timer starts automatically when you begin typing — useful for tracking call length.

Step 2 — Fill In the Client Section

Enter the client's name (used to personalize the email and SMS drafts), email, property address, credit score, scenario, and employment type. You don't need all of these to get useful numbers — start with what you have.

The Scenario dropdown (Debt Consolidation, Cash Out, Rate/Term, etc.) frames the situation and drives what the message drafts say on the right panel.

Step 3 — Enter Property & Mortgage Info

Enter the Home Value, Mortgage Balance, Mortgage Payment (with escrow), and Monthly Escrow. As you type, the LTV, CLTV, and max available equity at 80% and 90% calculate automatically at the bottom of that card.

Tip: Dollar fields support quick math — type 300+150 or =300+150 and it calculates inline. No need to pre-add numbers.

Step 4 — Cash & Debt

If the client wants to pull cash out, enter the amount in Cash Out. For debts to be paid off, use the Debts to Be Paid Off table — click + Add to add rows with liability type, balance, and monthly payment. Atlas totals the payoffs and eliminated payments and feeds them into each product's loan amount automatically.

The Monthly Debt field (or the itemized table) drives the current vs. new payment comparison shown in the Cash & Debt card.

Step 5 — Review the Products

The four product cards across the top — Fixed Rate Home Equity Loan, Interest Only HELOC, VA & Conventional Refi, and FHA Refi — update live as you enter data.

  • Use the checkbox on each card header to include or exclude that product.
  • Edit the Rate and Term fields directly on each card to match current pricing.
  • Click the ☆ star to mark a card as the recommended option — this highlights it and personalizes the message drafts.
  • Each card shows the loan amount (including financed closing costs), monthly payment, total housing cost, and savings vs. the client's current situation.

Step 6 — Closing Costs & VA

The Closing Cost Estimates panel (bottom-left) lets you set closing costs as a percentage or flat dollar amount, and adjust prepaid escrow months. These get financed into each product's loan amount automatically.

If applicable, check VA Loan to apply a VA funding fee, then select the correct fee type (IRRRL, Cash-Out First Use, etc.) from the dropdown.

Step 7 — Use the Right Panel

The tabs on the right generate ready-to-send communication based on everything you've entered:

  • Email — a full recap email personalized with the client's name, scenario, and the starred product.
  • SMS — a short text summary to send right after the call.
  • CRM — a structured internal note formatted for pasting into your CRM.

Hit Regenerate to refresh drafts any time you change inputs. Use Copy to copy the current draft, Email to Client to open it in Outlook, or Email to Myself to send a copy to yourself.

Internal Notes

The Internal Notes box (bottom-right of the main panel) is a scratchpad for anything you want to track that shouldn't appear in client-facing messages. It's never included in any draft.
